The Real Assessment Rate

Banks don't lend at the advertised rates you see on their websites. When assessing how much you can borrow, they apply a stress test buffer.

Assessment Rate (APRA Buffer)

7.10%

RBA Rate (4.10%) + 3% Serviceability Buffer

This is higher than current mortgage rates because banks want to know you can still repay if rates rise. The FHC uses this exact 7.10% assessment rate — the same one banks use.

Three Common Myths
❌ Myth 1: "I can borrow based on my salary"

Reality: Banks multiply your income by the assessment rate (7.10%), not the advertised rate. A $100k salary doesn't mean $600k+ borrowing power — it's closer to $350-400k depending on expenses. The FHC shows your real number.

❌ Myth 2: "20% deposit means I'm safe"

Reality: Deposit size is just one factor. Banks care more about your Debt-To-Income ratio (DTI) and serviceability. Even with 20%, you can be declined if your expenses are too high relative to income. The FHC breaks down all five factors.

❌ Myth 3: "Pre-approval means guaranteed"

Reality: Pre-approval is conditional. Banks re-assess everything at settlement — income, employment, credit file, everything. Conditions can change. Know your real position before you start hunting.
